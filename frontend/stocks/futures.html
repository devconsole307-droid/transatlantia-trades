What Are Futures Contracts?

A futures contract is a legal agreement to buy or sell an asset at a predetermined price at a specified time in the future. Unlike buying spot Bitcoin or stocks, futures let you control large positions with a fraction of the capital — this is called leverage.

If you believe Bitcoin will rise, you buy (go long) a BTC futures contract. If you expect it to fall, you sell (go short). Either way, you can profit from both directions of the market.

Pros & Cons of Futures Trading

The most powerful — and dangerous — instrument in trading. Here's why.

✅ Advantages
  • ⚡
    Amplified profit potentialLeverage means a 5% price move with 20x leverage equals a 100% return on your margin.
  • 📉
    Profit in bear marketsShort-selling futures lets you earn from falling prices — unlike traditional stock buying.
  • 🌍
    Access to global marketsTrade commodities, indices, currencies, and crypto all from one platform.
  • 💧
    Extremely liquid marketsBillions traded daily means your positions can be entered and exited instantly.
  • 🔒
    Hedging existing portfoliosFutures can protect your spot holdings from sharp market downturns.
⚠️ Risks & Challenges
  • 💥
    Liquidation riskHigh leverage means a small adverse move can wipe out your entire margin — liquidation is instant.
  • 📡
    Requires constant monitoringVolatile positions demand active oversight. Markets move fast and don't wait.
  • 🔄
    Funding rates eat profitsPerpetual futures contracts charge hourly funding fees that can erode long-term positions.
  • 🧠
    High complexityUnderstanding basis, contango, backwardation, and margin calls requires serious expertise.
  • 😰
    Psychological pressureWatching leveraged positions swing can cause panic decisions that destroy accounts.
