Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 1, 2026

1. Introduction

TransAtlantia Trades ("we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our platform at transatlantiatrades.com.

By using the Platform, you consent to the data practices described in this policy.

2. Information We Collect

CategoryData CollectedPurpose
Account DataName, email, phone, country, password (hashed)Account creation & authentication
KYC DocumentsGovernment ID, proof of address, selfieIdentity verification & compliance
Financial DataDeposit/withdrawal amounts, wallet addresses, transaction historyService delivery & fraud prevention
Usage DataIP address, browser type, pages visited, login timesSecurity monitoring & improvement
CommunicationsSupport chat messages, email correspondenceCustomer support

3. How We Use Your Information

  • To create and manage your account and process transactions
  • To verify your identity and comply with KYC/AML regulations
  • To send transactional emails (deposit confirmations, withdrawal updates, security alerts)
  • To detect, prevent, and investigate fraud and suspicious activity
  • To improve our platform and user experience
  • To respond to your support requests

4. Data Storage & Security

Your data is stored on secured servers with industry-standard encryption. We use bcrypt hashing for passwords and never store them in plain text. KYC documents are stored in access-controlled storage and are only accessible to authorized compliance personnel.

We implement the following security measures:

  • TLS/SSL encryption for all data in transit
  • Encrypted database storage for sensitive fields
  • JWT token-based authentication with expiry
  • Rate limiting to prevent brute-force attacks
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing

5. Data Sharing

We do not sell your personal information to third parties. We may share data with:

  • Regulatory authorities — when required by law or court order
  • Service providers — email delivery services, hosting providers (under strict confidentiality agreements)
  • Fraud prevention services — to detect and prevent financial crime

6. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your account and associated data
  • Object to certain processing of your data
  • Data portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@transatlantiatrades.com. Note that some requests may be limited by our legal obligations (e.g., we must retain financial records for regulatory compliance).

7. Cookies

We use localStorage (a browser-based storage mechanism) to maintain your login session. We do not use third-party advertising cookies. The Google Translate widget on our site may use cookies governed by Google's privacy policy.

8. Data Retention

We retain your account data for the duration of your account and for up to 5 years after account closure, as required by financial regulations. KYC documents are retained for a minimum of 5 years in accordance with AML compliance requirements.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. We will notify registered users of significant changes via email. Continued use of the Platform after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

10. Contact Us

For privacy-related questions: privacy@transatlantiatrades.com
For general support: support@transatlantiatrades.com
