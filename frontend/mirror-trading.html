What Exactly Is Mirror Trading?

Mirror trading — also known as copy trading — connects your account to a master trader's account. Every time the expert opens, adjusts, or closes a trade, the same action is automatically replicated in your account, proportional to your investment size.

Think of it as hiring a professional fund manager — except the system is fully automated, transparent, and you maintain complete control. You can start or stop mirroring at any time.

TransAtlantia's Mirror Trading system connects investors to our team of vetted expert traders across crypto, equities, and derivatives — with real-time performance metrics so you always know exactly who you're copying and how they're performing.

Pros & Cons of Mirror Trading

The most passive way to participate in professional trading — with important caveats.

✅ Advantages
  • 🧘
    Fully passive — zero effortNo analysis, no charts, no decisions. The expert does the work while profits flow to your account.
  • 🎓
    Learn while you earnWatch expert trades unfold in real time and absorb professional strategies without risking separate capital.
  • 📊
    Full transparencyEvery trade is visible — entry, exit, reasoning, and performance history are all accessible before you choose who to follow.
  • 🛡️
    Proven track recordsOnly audited traders with verified performance histories are available on our platform — no unverified claims.
  • 🔄
    Diversify across multiple tradersSplit your investment across different expert traders to reduce exposure to any single strategy.
⚠️ Risks & Challenges
  • 📉
    Past performance ≠ future resultsEven the best traders have drawdown periods. Historical returns are no guarantee of future profits.
  • 🤝
    Dependency on another personYour returns are tied to someone else's decisions. A bad streak by your chosen trader directly impacts you.
  • ⚙️
    Strategy drift riskTraders may change their approach over time, taking on more risk than their historical profile suggested.
  • 💸
    Performance feesTop traders typically charge a share of profits — which reduces your net return compared to the trader's gross performance.
  • 😴
    Complacency trapThe passive nature can cause investors to set-and-forget, ignoring when conditions change and it's time to re-evaluate.
